Where AI fits, and where it does not.

We spend our time helping AI-native founders decide what belongs on the algorithm and what belongs on the rest of the stack. The short version of how we think about it: most problems are not AI problems. Sixty percent are traditional code and database. Thirty percent are rule-based logic. Ten percent are the real machine-learning problem.

Inside that ten percent sits Chromatic's coefficient model. Around it sits the harder commercial work of supply-side density, design-partner contracts, and the path from the salon install base to a social-platform install base. The algorithm is the moat. The orchestration around it is the business.

The neighboring work.

Our cofounder published Ethics Engine on arXiv last year. Ethics Engine is a psychometric assessment tool for evaluating ideological and moral patterns in LLMs. It reads the same neighborhood as Chromatic's patent family: social-science structure applied to credibility at machine speed. The repo is MIT-licensed.

  • Paper arxiv.org/abs/2510.11742
  • Repo github.com/RinDig/AuditEngine

Chromatic evaluates human reviewers. Ethics Engine evaluates machines. The adjacency is the reason to talk, and the reason the conversation we want with Chromatic is a peer methodology conversation, not a vendor call.

The shape of the engagement.

Feeld's CTO Andrew Santus ran a scoped sprint with us last year. Workshop, advisory calls, an Organizational Context Architecture, a Strategic Operations Framework. The deliverable was a decision about which parts of his stack got AI, which parts stayed rules-based, and which parts did not get built at all.

That is the shape of engagement that fits a founder-and-chairman who already has the technology and is deciding where the company points next. It is a read on the wedge, not a production build.

What sits below the layer.

Eduba partners with NLP Logix for work that sits below the orchestration layer. NLP Logix has been in machine learning since 2011 and runs over 150 data scientists. That lane is not the first conversation here. If Chromatic ever chooses to scale a production credibility API into news platforms and needs an inference and data pipeline wrapped around the patented model, that conversation is month six. Month one is methodology.

Five questions we would bring to the first call.

  1. Where is the coefficient getting its supervision signal today. User-level history, platform-level heuristics, hand-labeled seeds, or something not yet described publicly.
  2. Which side of the wedge gets the team for the next 12 months. The SMB verticals shipping through the Curate App, or the social and news lane in the public roadmap.
  3. With NewsGuard and Originality.ai on the map, which of them reads as the near-term peer and which reads as the floor.
  4. On the three patents, defensive or offensive. A moat for the product, or a license to platforms that already run a review product.
  5. What does a Chromatic design-partner contract look like today, and what should it look like 12 months from now.
